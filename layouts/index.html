I teach Sociology at Duke University

I am Associate Professor of Sociology and the Kenan Institute for Ethics. My other local affiliations include the undergraduate program in Markets and Management Studies and the Duke Network Analysis Center.

My Vita (PDF) →

Much of my research has been about exchange in human blood and organs, cultural goods, software, and ideas. My current research focuses on the moral order of market society, the effect of models and measurement on social classification, and the link between those two topics, especially in the consumer credit market. I am also interested in techniques and methods for data visualization.
